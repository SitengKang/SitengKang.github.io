I am an Applied Scientist at Amazon Alexa working on large-scale language understanding and machine learning systems. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Illinois Chicago in 2025, where I was advised by [Prof. Xinhua Zhang](https://www.cs.uic.edu/~zhangx/). My research broadly centers on **trustworthy machine learning in temporal environments**, including reinforcement learning, continual learning, robust sequential decision-making, and large language models.

My work studies how learning systems behave under **non-stationarity, distribution shift, and adversarial perturbations**. In particular, I am interested in building models and training frameworks that remain reliable as data, environments, and objectives evolve over time. This includes research on **continual adaptation**, **robustness in reinforcement learning**, **poisoning in offline-to-online learning**, and **learning-based data acquisition strategies** for scientific machine learning.

At Amazon, I build large-scale intent understanding and multilingual language systems for Alexa, with recent work spanning **human-in-the-loop LLM data curation**, **scalable intent classification**, and **multilingual transfer** for production language understanding. More broadly, I am interested in bridging foundational machine learning research and real-world deployment, especially in settings where reliability, scale, and temporal dynamics all matter.

Before UIC, I worked with [Prof. Eli Upfal](https://cs.brown.edu/people/eupfal/) and [Dr. Cyrus Cousins](https://cs.brown.edu/~ccousins/) at Brown University, where I earned my M.S. in Computer Science. I received my B.S. in Mathematics and Computer Science from Penn State and also worked with [Prof. Jeremiah Blocki](https://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/jblocki/) as a visiting research assistant at Purdue University.
